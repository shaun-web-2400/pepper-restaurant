About

The Story

A small room with a long memory.

Pepper opened on a quiet stretch near the harbor with one idea: cook what the season gives, and cook it carefully. We started with eight tables and a single menu that changed every week. The room has grown a little since, but the idea has not. We still build each evening around one progression, still write the menu by hand, and still know most of the farmers by name.

Philosophy

Fewer things, done with intent.

Our cooking leans on restraint. A plate rarely holds more than three or four ideas, and each one has to earn its place. We let strong ingredients stay recognizable, so a roasted carrot tastes like a roasted carrot, and we build flavor through technique rather than excess.

The result is food that feels generous without being heavy, and refined without feeling fussy.

Executive Chef

Mara Ellison

Mara trained across kitchens in Lyon and the Pacific Northwest before returning home to Boston. Her cooking carries the discipline of classic French technique and the ingredient-first instinct of the coast she grew up on. She believes a tasting menu should tell a single story from first bite to last.

"Good food is mostly editing, knowing what to leave off the plate."

Sustainability

Sourced close, used fully.

We buy from a small group of regional farms and day-boat fishers, which keeps our menu honest about what is actually in season. In the kitchen we work toward as little waste as possible. Trim becomes stock, off-cuts become staff meal, and scraps head to compost with a partner farm. It is not a marketing program. It is just how a small kitchen should run.
